# Email Draft Template

Use this template for items in the **Emails** section (replies not tied to a decision coversheet).

## Template

```markdown
### Reply: [Subject] -- to [Recipient Name]

**Summary**: [One line: what the email is about and why a reply is needed]
**Recommended tone**: [e.g., "Warm acknowledgment", "Firm but collegial decline", "Quick scheduling"]

<details><summary>Original email excerpt</summary>

From: [sender]
Date: [date]
Subject: [subject]

> [Key excerpt -- enough to understand what needs a reply, not the whole email]

</details>

**Draft**:

To: recipient@example.com
Subject: Re: [Original subject]
In-Reply-To: [entry_id]

Hi [Name],

[Draft text]

Best,
Nic

- [ ] Sent

<!-- @nic: send / send as edited / redraft - [feedback] -->
```

## Voice conventions

- **Salutation**: "Hi [Name]," (never "Dear")
- **Sign-off**: "Best," or "Cheers," (never "Best regards" or "Kind regards")
- **Tone**: Warm, direct, collegial -- like talking to a respected peer over coffee
- **Non-responders**: When someone hasn't replied to a previous email, use a more tentative, warmer tone -- not transactional
- **Length**: Match the complexity of the reply needed. Simple acknowledgments can be 1-2 sentences.

## Rules

1. **Full drafts, not talking points.** Composition friction is the enemy -- a draft that needs editing is 10x better than bullet points that need writing.
2. **Include `In-Reply-To: [entry_id]`** so the process-bundle skill can thread the reply correctly.
3. **Include the original email excerpt** in a collapsed details section for context.
4. **One draft per email.** If the decision requires choosing between responses, use a decision coversheet instead.
