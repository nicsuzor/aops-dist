# Peer Review Detailed Criteria

Detailed procedures and checklists for peer review activities, including grant and fellowship reviews.

## Grant Review Criteria

When reviewing grant applications (e.g., ARC, SNSF), focus on:

- **Significance and Innovation**: Is the project important? Does it push the field forward?
- **Approach and Methodology**: Is the plan sound? Are the methods appropriate?
- **Investigator(s) / Team**: Does the team have the expertise and track record?
- **Environment**: Does the host institution provide necessary support?

## Fellowship Review Criteria

For fellowship applications (e.g., ARC Future Fellowships):

- **Candidate Quality**: Track record relative to opportunity, leadership potential.
- **Project Merit**: Significance, innovation, and benefit.
- **Strategic Alignment**: Alignment with national/institutional research priorities.

## Submission Checklists

Before final submission, verify:

- [ ] All required fields are completed.
- [ ] Word/character limits are respected.
- [ ] Tone is professional, constructive, and objective.
- [ ] Conflicts of interest have been declared/checked.
- [ ] Scores align with the textual feedback provided.

## Platform-Specific Instructions

### ARC (Research Management System - RMS)

- Ensure browser compatibility.
- Save frequently (auto-save is not always reliable).
- Check for PDF rendering of the proposal.

### SNSF (mySNF)

- Follow specific Swiss National Science Foundation guidelines.
- Pay attention to the "Scientific Relevance" section.
